About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Swansea University, Wales (UK), working on magnetically controlled aligned and oriented H₂ reactive scattering on metal surfaces. My research focuses on stereodynamics, quantum-state–resolved molecule–surface interactions, and energy-transfer mechanisms in gas–surface reactions. Alongside my academic work, I have extensive hands-on experience in scientific instrumentation with strong industrial relevance. During my PhD, I designed and developed a high-precision, low-cost wavemeter (patented, DAE–TIFR) and built a sub-ppb stable He-Ne laser using advanced feedback electronics. I also designed, optimized, and characterized a TOF mass spectrometer, including spatial/velocity-map imaging modes for surface scattering experiments.I have developed a compact, high-flux, highly collimated helium beam source for scattering measurements, with potential applications in areas such as thin-film growth on atomically flat surfaces and other precision surface-science techniques. My broader technical expertise spans mass-spectrometry ion optics, beam instrumentation, UHV vacuum engineering, SIMION-based charged-particle simulations, and complete instrument development, from conceptual modelling and design to assembly, calibration, and data analysis. I also have strong experience in experiment automation and control using LabVIEW, Python, MATLAB, and microcontrollers. I am interested in opportunities in advanced R&D, scientific instrumentation, mass spectrometry, ion optics, vacuum technologies, and analytical instrument development.

Research Overview

My research spans molecular beam scattering, quantum state preparation, surface reaction dynamics, and high-resolution laser spectroscopy. I investigate how molecular orientation, alignment, and vibrational state influence reactivity on metal surfaces, using advanced interferometric and scattering methods.
